## Page 2 — Introduction

Most people start in the wrong place with AI.

They chase tools.
They test prompts.
They generate a few outputs.
Then they wonder why nothing really changes.

That’s because using AI is not the same as deploying AI.

If you want real leverage, you need to stop thinking in terms of tools and start thinking in terms of roles.

That means asking better questions:
- What job should AI own?
- What kind of work is repetitive enough to delegate?
- What role would create immediate relief inside my business or workflow?
- What boundaries would make that role useful and safe?

That is what this Starter Kit is for.

In the next few pages, you’ll do four things:
1. choose the best first AI employee role
2. define the job clearly
3. identify the first tasks to assign
4. set simple guardrails before you deploy

By the end, you’ll have more than a vague idea.
You’ll have a real starting point.

And that matters, because the first AI employee you build shapes how you think about AI from this point forward.

Let’s make sure your first one is useful.

## Page 4 — Score the Roles

# Step 2: Score Each Role

Rate each role from **1–5** in the following categories:

### Repetition
How often does this task happen?

### Clarity
How clearly can you explain the work?

### Frequency
Does it happen often enough to create meaningful leverage?

### Business Value
Would solving this actually save time, reduce drag, or improve output?

### Risk
How safe is it if the AI gets it partially wrong?

### Reviewability
Can a human review the output quickly?
